Optional Practical Training (OPT) for F-1 Students

Overview:
Optional Practical Training (OPT) is temporary employment directly related to an F-1 student's major area of study. OPT is available to F-1 students before and/or after completion of their degree.

Types of OPT:
1. Pre-Completion OPT: Available while still enrolled in school. Limited to 20 hours/week during the academic year, full-time during breaks.
2. Post-Completion OPT: Available after completing a degree. 12 months of full-time employment authorization.

Eligibility:
- Must be in valid F-1 status
- Must have been enrolled full-time for at least one academic year
- Employment must be directly related to major area of study
- Must not have used 12 or more months of full-time CPT (Curricular Practical Training)

Application Timeline:
- Earliest to apply: 90 days before program end date
- Latest to apply: 60 days after program end date
- OPT start date must be within 60 days after program completion
- Processing time: Typically 3-5 months (can vary)

Application Process:
1. Request OPT recommendation from your DSO (Designated School Official)
2. DSO updates your SEVIS record with OPT recommendation
3. DSO issues new I-20 with OPT recommendation on page 2
4. File Form I-765 (Application for Employment Authorization) with USCIS
5. Include filing fee, passport photos, and supporting documents
6. Wait for EAD (Employment Authorization Document) card

Important Rules:
- You CANNOT work until you receive your EAD card
- You must report employment information to your DSO within 10 days
- Maximum 90 days of unemployment allowed during 12-month OPT period
- Unemployment days count from your OPT start date (not EAD receipt date)
- Each period of unemployment counts toward the 90-day limit
- If you exceed 90 days of unemployment, your F-1 status is terminated

Reporting Requirements:
- Report employer name and address to DSO
- Report any change of employer within 10 days
- Report any interruption of employment within 10 days
- Update address with DSO and USCIS if you move

Filing Fee: $410 (as of 2024, subject to change)

Grace Period:
- After OPT ends, you have a 60-day grace period
- During grace period, you CANNOT work
- Use this time to prepare departure, transfer to new school, or change status
