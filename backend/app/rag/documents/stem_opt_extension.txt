STEM OPT Extension (24-Month Extension)

Overview:
The STEM OPT extension allows F-1 students with degrees in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics) fields to extend their post-completion OPT by an additional 24 months, for a total of 36 months of work authorization.

Eligibility Requirements:
- Must have a bachelor's, master's, or doctoral degree in a STEM field listed on the DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List
- Must currently be on an approved period of post-completion OPT
- Must be employed by an employer enrolled in E-Verify
- Employer and student must complete Form I-983 (Training Plan)
- Must apply before current OPT expires

E-Verify Requirement:
- Your employer MUST be enrolled in E-Verify
- This is non-negotiable â€” no exceptions
- You can verify employer E-Verify status through the E-Verify portal
- Self-employment is NOT allowed on STEM OPT

Application Timeline:
- Apply up to 90 days before your current OPT EAD expires
- Your OPT is automatically extended for up to 180 days while your STEM extension is pending
- File Form I-765 with category (c)(3)(C)

Form I-983 Training Plan Requirements:
- Must describe the STEM training opportunity
- Must explain how the training relates to your STEM degree
- Must include learning objectives and a training plan
- Employer must sign and commit to training objectives
- Must be updated every 6 months (self-evaluation)
- Must be updated if you change employers
- Must be submitted to DSO within 10 days of start

Employment Rules on STEM OPT:
- Must work at least 20 hours per week
- Can work for more than one employer (each must be E-Verify enrolled)
- Must report any change in employer to DSO within 10 days
- Must report material changes to training plan
- Self-employment is NOT permitted
- Volunteering does not count as employment

Unemployment Limits:
- Maximum 150 days of aggregate unemployment during the entire 36-month OPT + STEM OPT period
- This includes any unemployment days from the initial 12-month OPT
- Exceeding 150 days terminates your F-1 status

Reporting Requirements:
- Self-evaluation to DSO every 6 months
- Report employer name, address, and EIN to DSO
- Validate I-20 information annually
- Report any name or address changes within 10 days
- Final evaluation upon completion of STEM OPT

Employer Obligations:
- Must be enrolled in E-Verify
- Must attest that STEM OPT worker is not replacing a US worker
- Must provide training and supervision as outlined in I-983
- Must report termination of student to DSO within 5 days
- Must allow DHS site visits for compliance verification
